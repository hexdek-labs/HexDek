===============================================================
  MTGSQUAD / HEXDEK — UI DESIGN SPECIFICATION
  Full Screen Mockups, Flow, and Visual Direction
===============================================================

VISUAL IDENTITY
═══════════════

BRAND:     mtgsquad (product), HexDek (engine brand)
FEEL:      Dark forge. Norse-industrial. The blacksmith's
           workshop where weapons get made, not the showroom.
PALETTE:
  Background:    #0A0A0F (near-black with blue undertone)
  Surface:       #14141F (cards, panels, modals)
  Surface-hover: #1E1E2E (elevated panels)
  Primary:       #4F8FFF (ice blue — Hex's signature)
  Accent:        #FF6B35 (forge orange — for actions, CTAs)
  Success:       #22C55E (green — combos found, tests pass)
  Warning:       #FBBF24 (amber — caution states)
  Danger:        #EF4444 (red — failures, critical alerts)
  Text-primary:  #E4E4EF (soft white, not blinding)
  Text-muted:    #6B7280 (gray for secondary info)
  Gold:          #D4A843 (for legendary/mythic borders)

TYPOGRAPHY:
  Headlines:   JetBrains Mono or Space Grotesk (technical, clean)
  Body:        Inter (readable at small sizes)
  Data/Stats:  JetBrains Mono (monospaced for numbers, tables)
  Card names:  Beleren (if licensable) or Cinzel (serif, arcane)

TEXTURE:
  Subtle noise grain on backgrounds (5% opacity)
  Thin 1px borders on panels (#1E1E2E)
  No gradients except for score meters
  No rounded corners > 8px — angular, forged
  Glow effects on interactive elements (box-shadow with primary)
  Card art: frosted glass panels over blurred card art as
  section backgrounds (when showing a specific card/deck)

MOTIF:
  Norse runes as decorative elements (subtle, in borders)
  The frog logo (BlueFrog parent brand) occasionally appears
  as a tiny watermark — not dominant
  Hexagonal grid pattern as a background texture on data screens
  Forge/anvil imagery for processing/building states

ANIMATION:
  Minimal. Quick fades (150ms). No bouncing, no slides.
  Loading states: forge ember particle effect (small, subtle)
  Data streaming in: typewriter effect on numbers counting up
  Card hover: slight Y-axis tilt (3D perspective, 2-3 degrees)

===============================================================
INTERACTION DESIGN NOTES
===============================================================

CARD INTERACTIONS:
- Hover: card tilts slightly (3D CSS transform), shows glow
- Click: expands to full card with oracle text + stats overlay
- Long press (mobile): same as hover
- Drag: used only during gameplay for attacking/blocking
- Cards in hand fan out like a real hand of cards

TRANSITIONS:
- Screen changes: quick fade (150ms), no sliding
- Modal open: scale from 0.95 to 1.0 + fade
- Data loading: skeleton screens with pulse animation
- Game actions: brief flash on affected cards (100ms highlight)

SOUNDS (optional, off by default):
- Card draw: soft paper slide
- Spell cast: subtle arcane tone
- Combat damage: impact thud
- Combo assembled: ascending chime sequence
- Game win: satisfying chord
- Critical moment: low rumble (subwoofer territory)

ACCESSIBILITY:
- All text meets WCAG AA contrast on dark backgrounds
- Card colors supplemented with icons (not color-only)
- Screen reader labels on all interactive elements
- Keyboard navigation for entire game board
- Reduced motion setting: disables all animations

VIBES SUMMARY
===============================================================

Think: if Discord's dark theme, Bloomberg Terminal, and a
Viking blacksmith's workshop had a baby that played Magic.

NOT: Arena (too polished/corporate), MTGO (too ugly/dated),
Moxfield (too utilitarian), Archidekt (too busy).

The aesthetic says: "This was built by engineers who play
Magic, not designers who were told to make a Magic app."

Data-dense but not cluttered. Dark but not depressing.
Technical but not intimidating. The kind of UI where seeing
raw numbers feels like power, not homework.

Every screen should make you feel like you're looking at
something most people don't get to see — the internals of
how Magic actually works, quantified and visualized.

The forge metaphor runs throughout: you're forging decks
into weapons, forging yourself into a better player, and the
engine is the forge fire that makes it all possible.
